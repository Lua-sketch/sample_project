What I do

When I'm not in school, I have a few ways to keep myself occupied. I am a volunteer TA in Robotics For All, currently in a Lego Explorers and a code.org class. I take part in a training program for mathematics olympiads in a local university, where I was also invited to take Number Theory with their undergrads. I couldn't enroll in other courses due to the pandemic. I have a coding club for girls and write a column in the school newspaper about women in STEM. Every year, I participate in the Breakthrough Junior Challenge with physics video submissions. Currently, I'm participating in Qubit by Qubit, a quantum computing course which made me want to code. I began coding independently, with HTML/CSS, then Python, Lua, and Processing. Now I'm reviewing HTML/CSS and learning JS. In my free time, I play the guitar. Finally, I'm currently studying wormholes and trying to write a review article on whether transversable wormholes are a feasible prospect for a time travel technology.
